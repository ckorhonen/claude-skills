# Source Signals

This file captures the design cues derived from the analyzed source material so another agent can stay close to the visual logic without copying it literally.

## Primary sources

- [Pedro Fleming, "Neon Genesis Evangelion Screen Graphics"](https://www.pedrofleming.com/neongenesisevangelion)
- [Astromono, "The Amazing UI Design of Evangelion"](https://astromono.wordpress.com/2015/06/04/ui-design-of-evangelion/)

## Signals from the text

- The Pedro Fleming write-up frames Evangelion UI as a storytelling device for tension, constant threat, and worldbuilding, not simple decoration.
- Fleming emphasizes fidelity to researched source material plus reinterpretation, which is the right model for this skill: understand the grammar, then create an original screen.
- The Astromono article highlights simple geometric shapes, fast-paced animation, and data-derived forms such as Venn-like structures and Gaussian or curvature-based visuals.
- Both sources imply the same standard: the interface should look researched and intentional, not randomly futuristic.

## Signals from the images

- Panoramic HUD masks float over subdued scenery instead of filling the whole frame with chrome.
- Many screens use black or near-black fields with thin warm outlines and isolated hot numerals.
- Charts and diagnostics rely on rings, waveforms, stacked bars, axes, and matrix layouts.
- Warning views use red substrates, amber numerals, and green confirmation labels to create semantic contrast.
- Title-card treatments are extremely sparse: black background, stacked typography, one accent, almost no decoration.
- Dense panels still preserve strong containment and alignment, so the data feels engineered rather than messy.

## Signals from the motion

- Countdowns tick in discrete steps.
- Scanners sweep horizontally or radially.
- Panels blink on, off, or swap states abruptly.
- Lock-on and targeting graphics converge with precision rather than elastic easing.
- Motion is brief and informational. It exists to confirm state changes, not to entertain.

## Translation rules

- Preserve the emotional function: surveillance, threat, synchronization, command, diagnosis.
- Preserve the structural language: precise geometry, hard containment, constrained color.
- Change the actual composition, copy, and assets so the outcome is an original homage rather than a replica.
- If the design starts to feel like generic cyberpunk or a gaming HUD skin, pull it back toward discipline, negative space, and system-state clarity.
